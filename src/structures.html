Famous structures

The world is full of wonderful examples of masterpieces made of marble. We would like to present you a few

Architecture

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a monument in the city of Agra, India. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The building was built between 1630 and 1653. Designed by Iranian architect Ustad Isa, the Taj Mahal has become "the symbol of eternal love." The building "rises" from the reddish landscape, sitting on a white marble terrace that highlights the huge dome and the four minarets.

The tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa, also known as the Leaning Tower of Pisa, is the most famous leaning building in the world and the landmark of the city of Pisa in Italy. It differs from the usual square towers in central Italy and is in stark contrast to the sharp towers in northern Europe. It consists of 14,200 tons of white Carrara marble and has seven bells, but they have not been allowed to ring for a long time due to the danger of collapse.

Supreme Court Building in Washington

The Supreme Court of the United States of America is the supreme court of the United States judiciary. The four-story building was designed by Cass Gilbert in a classic style sympathetic to the surrounding buildings of the Chapter and Library of Congress and is clad in marble. The building includes the courtroom, courtrooms, an extensive law library, various meeting spaces and ancillary services, including a gym.

Sculptures

Pietà by Michelangelo

Pietà is a beautiful marble sculpture by the great Michelangelo Buonarroti, which is now preserved in the St. Peter’s Basilica in the Vatican City.The statue was made for the French cardinal Jean de Billheres, who had been assigned to Rome.

Moses by Michelangelo

"Moses" is a 235 cm high marble statue of the Old Testament prophet. It depicts the biblical figure Moses with horns on his head, based on a description in chapter 34 of Exodus in the Vulgate, the Latin translation of the Bible used at that time, which occupies the central place in the sculptural tomb of Pope Julius II in the Roman basilica San Pietro in Vincoli. Michelangelo worked on this sculpture between 1513-1515.

Discobolus

The Discobolus of Myron is a Greek sculpture completed at the start of the Classical period, figuring a youthful athlete throwing discus, about 460–450 BC. The original Greek bronze is lost but the work is known through numerous Roman copies, both full-scale ones in marble, which was cheaper than bronze, such as the first to be recovered, the Palombara Discobolus, and smaller scaled versions in bronze.
